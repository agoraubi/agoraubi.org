## 2. Why Bitcoin's Solution Isn't Enough

### Bitcoin's Revolutionary Insight

In 2008, Satoshi Nakamoto solved the fundamental problem of digital money:

> *"What is needed is an electronic payment system based on cryptographic proof instead of trust."*

Bitcoin showed that **money doesn't need central banks.**

**Bitcoin's achievements:**
- Decentralized creation
- Fixed supply (21M cap)
- Censorship-resistant
- Trustless verification
- Borderless transfers

### But Bitcoin Has Limitations

**1. Centralization Through Wealth**

The more money you have, the more:
- Mining equipment you can buy
- Hash power you control
- Bitcoin you can accumulate
- Influence you wield

**Result:** Bitcoin mining is dominated by large corporations. The dream of "one CPU, one vote" is dead.

**2. Tied to Fiat System**

To get Bitcoin, most people must:
- Buy it with fiat currency
- Through centralized exchanges
- Subject to KYC/AML regulations
- At prices determined by speculative trading

**Result:** Bitcoin remains **entangled with the very system it sought to replace.**

**3. Speculation Over Usage**

Bitcoin's "store of value" narrative:
- Encourages hoarding, not spending
- Creates volatile price swings
- Makes it impractical as daily currency
- Benefits early adopters at expense of latecomers

**Result:** Bitcoin became digital gold - an investment vehicle, not a medium of exchange.

### The Core Problem

**You can't escape fiat by using fiat to enter crypto.**

As long as access requires existing wealth:
- The wealth gap persists
- Central institutions maintain control
- Economic freedom remains elusive
- Power structures don't change

**We need money that's accessible to everyone, regardless of current wealth.**

### Identity & Privacy

**Q: How do you prevent fake accounts?**  
A: AGORA uses a dual-layer verification system:

1. **Civic Pass** (Primary): Government ID + live selfie verification through Civic, the leading identity solution on Solana with 2M+ verifications. Your personal data stays off-chain - only a cryptographic attestation is stored on Solana.

2. **Biometric Hash** (Secondary): During registration, a fingerprint hash is captured. This prevents the same person from registering multiple times with different wallets. The hash is one-way - cannot be reversed to actual fingerprint.

**Q: Is my identity public?**  
A: No. Personal data (name, ID photo, document numbers) is stored securely OFF-chain by Civic. On the blockchain, we store only:
- Your wallet address (pseudonymous)
- A Civic Gateway Token (proves you're verified, not WHO you are)
- A biometric hash (proves uniqueness, cannot identify you)

**Q: What if I don't have a government digital ID?**  
A: Civic Pass accepts various government-issued IDs:
- Passports (recommended)
- National ID cards
- Driver's licenses (in supported countries)

Initially AGORA focuses on regions with strong ID infrastructure. We'll expand to other verification methods (biometric-only, social graph) over time as the protocol matures.

**Q: What verification level does AGORA require?**  
A: AGORA requires "ID + Liveness" verification - the highest level Civic offers. This ensures:
- Your government ID is real and valid
- The live selfie matches the ID photo
- You are currently in possession of the ID (not using someone else's)

**Q: Can the government track my AGORA transactions?**  
A: Transactions are public on the blockchain (like Bitcoin), but not linked to your real identity - only to your wallet address. Civic does not share your personal data with AGORA or any third party without your consent.

**Q: How long does verification take?**  
A: Typically 2-5 minutes:
1. Upload government ID (~30 seconds)
2. Take live selfie (~30 seconds)
3. AI verification (~1-3 minutes)
4. Gateway Token issued on-chain (~10 seconds)

**Q: What if Civic rejects my verification?**  
A: Common reasons for rejection:
- Blurry or obscured ID photo
- Selfie doesn't match ID (lighting, angle issues)
- Expired government ID
- Unsupported ID type for your country

You can retry verification after addressing the issue. Civic provides specific feedback on why verification failed.
